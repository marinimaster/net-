# Maximum Tolerable Downtime (MTD)
Maximum time a business process can be unavailable before the impact becomes unacceptable.

# Recovery Time Objective (RTO)
Target time to restore the service after a disruption.

# Work Recovery Time (WRT)
Additional time needed after technical recovery to validate systems and resume normal work.

RTO + WRT cannot be greater than MTD.

# Recovery Point Objective (RPO)
Maximum acceptable data loss, usually measured as time.

Example:

An RPO of 24 hours means that if a database is destroyed by malware, it should be
restored from backup to a point not older than 24 hours.

Any data lost between the restored point and the present must either be accepted as a loss
or be reconstructed.
