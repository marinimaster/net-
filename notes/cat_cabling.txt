# Category (Cat) Cabling Standards

1. Cat 5
    * Speed: 100 Mbps (Fast Ethernet)
    * Frequency: 100 MHz
    * Max Distance: 100 meters

2. Cat 5e (Enhanced)
    * Speed: 1 Gbps (Gigabit Ethernet)
    * Frequency: 100 MHz
    * Max Distance: 100 meters
    * Note: Reduced crosstalk compared to Cat 5.

3. Cat 6
    * Speed: 1 Gbps (at 100m) / 10 Gbps (at 37-55m)
    * Frequency: 250 MHz
    * Max Distance: 100 meters (for 1 Gbps)
    * Note: Often includes a plastic separator (spline) to reduce interference.

4. Cat 6a (Augmented)
    * Speed: 10 Gbps
    * Frequency: 500 MHz
    * Max Distance: 100 meters
    * Note: Supports 10 Gbps over the full 100m distance.

5. Cat 7 (Class F)
    * Speed: 10 Gbps
    * Frequency: 600 MHz
    * Max Distance: 100 meters
    * Note: Individual shielding for each pair (S/FTP) to virtually eliminate crosstalk.

6. Cat 8
    * Speed: 40 Gbps
    * Frequency: 2000 MHz
    * Max Distance: 30 meters
    * Note: Designed for data centers and short-range high-speed links.

# Important Exam Concepts
* Shielded (STP) vs Unshielded (UTP): STP has a foil shield to protect against EMI; UTP is more common and flexible.
* Plenum vs PVC: Plenum-rated cable has special insulation that produces low smoke and non-toxic fumes (required for air-handling spaces).
* Termination: T568A and T568B wiring standards.
