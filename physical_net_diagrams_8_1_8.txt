- Physical Network Diagrams -
	Asset and configuration item (CI) documentation makes significant use of diagrams.
	A diagram is the best way to capture complex relationships between network elements.
	However, it is better not to include too much information if you want to keep a diagram useful.

- Cable Maps / Floor Plan -
	Shows how wires are routed through conduit from telecommunications closets to work areas.
	
	* Physically accurate floor plans are hard to design and are likely to 
	require the help of an architect or graphics professional.

Port Naming Conventions
	Alphanumeric (Identifiers for campus - Building - Telecommunications Space - Space - Port)
						CB01-01A-D01
	Main Campus Building (CB01), telecommunications space A on floor 1 (01A), data port 1 (D01)

i) In addition to having a diagram, it can be very useful to have a picture of the current config
   to provide a visual reference for troubleshooting and identifying unauthorized changes.

- Wiring Diagram -
	Shows detailed information about the termination of twisted pairs in an RJ45 jack or insulation
	Displacement Connector (IDC). You might also use a wiring diagram to document how fiber optic strands
	are terminated.

i) You should document the wiring diagrams used to terminate twisted pairs.
   
   Ethernet is wired using T568A or T568B, and the same standard should be used consistently throughout the
   network.

- Rack Diagrams -
	Records the position of each appliance in the rack.
	
	You can obtain stencils that represent vendor equipment from vendor websites or collections such as
	VisioCafe.
